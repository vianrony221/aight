Variations:

The Atari 2600, launched in 1977, revolutionized home gaming with its interchangeable cartridges and a vast library of titles. Known for its woodgrain design, the original model became iconic in the gaming world. It featured simple yet addictive games like Space Invaders and Pac-Man, bringing arcade experiences into living rooms. The Atari 2600 Vader (1982) introduced a sleek, all-black design, while the Atari 2600 Jr. (1986) offered a more compact and affordable version, making classic gaming accessible to a new generation. With its joystick controllers and groundbreaking technology, the Atari 2600 remains a symbol of the golden age of video games.

Features


  • Processor: MOS 6507 CPU running at 1.19 MHz.
  • Memory: 128 bytes of RAM and 4 KB of ROM in the console.
  • Graphics: Custom TIA (Television Interface Adapter) chip, capable of displaying simple graphics with a resolution of 160 x 192 pixels.
  • Sound: TIA chip also handles audio, with 2-bit sound output.
  • Games: Cartridge-based system with interchangeable game cartridges.
  • Controllers: Joystick controllers with a single button and a rotary knob (later models included paddles and other peripherals).
  • Video Output: RF (radio frequency) output, which required an RF modulator to connect to the TV.
  • Power Supply: External power adapter providing 9V DC.
  • Dimensions: Approximately 15.5 inches x 10.5 inches x 3.5 inches (39.4 cm x 26.7 cm x 8.9 cm).
  • Weight: Around 3.5 pounds (1.6 kg).
  • Design: Distinctive wood-grain panel and black plastic casing.
  • Cartridges: Game cartridges varied in size and complexity; popular titles included Pong, Space Invaders, and Pac-Man.
