The Nintendo Game Boy was a revolutionary handheld gaming console released in 1989. It featured a monochrome 2.45-inch LCD screen with a resolution of 160 x 144 pixels. Powered by a Sharp LR35902 processor running at 4.19 MHz and 8 KB of internal RAM, the Game Boy offered an impressive gaming experience for its time. The system used cartridges to play games, which allowed for a diverse library of titles including classics like Tetris, Super Mario Land, and The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening.

Features


  • Display: 2.6-inch monochrome LCD screen with a 160 x 144 pixel resolution.
  • Processor: 8-bit Z80-like CPU running at 4.19 MHz.
  • Memory: 8 KB of RAM and 256 bytes of VRAM.
  • Storage: Cartridge-based games with removable game cartridges.
  • Audio: Built-in 4-channel stereo sound.
  • Battery Life: Powered by 4 AA batteries, providing approximately 15-30 hours of playtime.
  • Dimensions: 90 mm x 148 mm x 32 mm (3.6 in x 5.8 in x 1.3 in).
  • Weight: Approximately 220 grams (7.8 ounces).
  • Controls: D-pad, A and B buttons, Start and Select buttons.
  • Connectivity: Link Cable port for multiplayer gaming.
